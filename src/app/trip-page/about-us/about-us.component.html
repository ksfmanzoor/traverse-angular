About

Tucked away in the Karakorams, lies the Hunza Valley, preserving its culture in its every beating heart. The sound of the rhubab, a local instrument, echoes against the Ultar Sar (7,388m), Hunza Peak (6,270m) the Lady Finger (6,000m) as the sun rises and sets around Eagles Nest. Each day begins and ends as the sky is painted with hues merging and blending harmoniously around Rakaposhi, Spantik and Ultar Sar the grand 7000m giants. Brushuski, one of the best known linguistic isolates, is the spoken language of the local Brusho people, the personification of the warmest of hosts. The landscape unlike any in the world, glistens.The landscape.
